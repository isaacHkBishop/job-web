Demand & Job Market Outlook

  • The overall demand for network/network-operations/network-engineer jobs in the U.S. remains positive
  • According to one estimate, there are tens of thousands of active job openings for network engineer / network operations roles nationwide
  • Growth projections: for roles roughly in the “network administrator / network engineer / network operations” space, employment growth is projected to be about 5% over a decade (e.g. 2018–2028) — roughly average compared to all occupations
  • For more senior/architect-level roles — e.g. Computer Network Architects — growth is stronger: projected ~12% growth from 2024–2034
  • Underlying drivers: as companies continue to expand and secure their IT infrastructure — including cloud adoption, increased data flow, remote work, cybersecurity needs — demand for experienced network professionals remains significant

    • Salary Expectations

  • Entry-Level/Junior Network Engineer: $55,000 - $75,000 annually
  • Mid-Level Network Engineer: $85,000 - $110,000 annually
  • Senior Network Engineer: $120,000 - $160,000+ annually
  • Network Architect: $110,000 - $160,000+ annually
  • many variables play a role (location, certifications, specialization, company size, cloud/secure networking, etc.)

    • Key Skills Employers / the Market Want

  • Proficiency in networking protocols (TCP/IP, BGP, OSPF, etc.)
  • Experience with network hardware (routers, switches, firewalls)
  • Knowledge of network security principles and practices
  • Familiarity with cloud networking (AWS, Azure, GCP)
  • Troubleshooting and problem-solving skills
  • Scripting/automation skills (Python, Ansible, etc.)
  • Certifications: Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA) or CompTIA Network+

    Challenges & what to Watch Out For

  • Not all “network engineer” jobs are equal — titles and responsibilities vary. Some roles might just be basic administration/support, which are lower-paying
  • The pay range is broad. Without specialization or experience, you may end up in lower-paying roles
  • The job market can feel competitive and at times unpredictable — some professionals (on forums) report slow hiring or difficulty getting callbacks even with good credentials (especially if they try to enter the market mid-career or after layoffs)
  • As infrastructure shifts (cloud, automation), some traditional networking tasks evolve — meaning ongoing learning is often required.
